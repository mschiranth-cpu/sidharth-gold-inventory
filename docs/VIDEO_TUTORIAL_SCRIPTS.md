## Tutorial 2: Creating and Tracking Orders (10 minutes)

### Title: "Mastering Order Management"

---

**[0:00 - 0:30] Introduction**

*Visual: Orders list view*

**Narrator:**

---

**[0:30 - 2:30] Creating a New Order**

*Visual: Create Order form*

**Narrator:**
"To create a new order, click Orders in the sidebar, then click the 'New Order' button.

First, enter the customer details. The customer name is required - this could be a company like 'ABC Jewelers' or an individual name.

Next, enter the product details:
- **Product Name**: What are we making? For example, 'Gold Necklace 22K'
- **Quantity**: How many pieces?
- **Gold Weight**: Total weight in grams
- **Purity**: Select from 24K, 22K, 18K, or 14K

Set the **Due Date** - when does the customer need this?

Choose the **Priority**:
- Low for flexible timelines
- Medium is the default
- High for important customers
- Urgent for rush orders

Finally, add any special notes. This is important - include design specs, customer preferences, or special instructions.

Click Create Order, and you're done!"

## Tutorial 3: Department Workflow (8 minutes)

### Title: "Understanding the Production Flow"

---

**[0:00 - 0:30] Introduction**

*Visual: Factory workflow diagram*

**Narrator:**
"In this tutorial, we'll explore how orders flow through departments and how each team member contributes to the process."

---

**[0:30 - 2:00] The Production Journey**

*Visual: Animated workflow diagram*

**Narrator:**
"Every order follows a defined path through the factory:

1. **Receiving** - Order enters the system
2. **Melting** - Gold is purified and prepared
3. **Designing** - Patterns and molds created
4. **Moulding** - Gold shaped into product
5. **Polishing** - Surface finishing
6. **Quality Check** - Inspection and approval
7. **Packaging** - Prepared for delivery
8. **Dispatch** - Sent to customer

This workflow ensures consistent quality and complete tracking."

---

**[2:00 - 3:30] Department Worker View**

*Visual: Department-specific dashboard*

**Narrator:**
"As a department worker, your view is focused on your specific area.

Your 'My Department' page shows only the orders currently in your department. The queue is sorted by priority and due date.

To work on an order, click to open it. You'll see all the information you need - specs, notes from previous departments, and any special instructions.

When you complete your task, click 'Update Status'. Add notes about your work - this helps the next department and creates a valuable record."

*Action: Walk through worker workflow*

---

**[3:30 - 5:00] Moving Orders (Manager View)**

*Visual: Manager Kanban with drag-drop*

**Narrator:**
"Factory Managers control the flow between departments.

On the Kanban board, simply drag an order card to the next department. The system will:
- Update the order status
- Log the movement with timestamp
- Notify the receiving department
- Update all dashboards in real-time

You can also use the 'Move Order' button if you prefer. This gives you the option to add notes about the transfer."

*Action: Demonstrate both methods*

---

**[5:00 - 6:30] Handling Issues**

*Visual: Issue reporting flow*

**Narrator:**
"Sometimes things don't go as planned. The system has built-in issue handling.

If a worker encounters a problem - maybe a material defect or equipment issue - they can click 'Report Issue'. Select the issue type, add a description, and submit.

The factory manager is immediately notified and can decide the next steps:
- Send back to previous department
- Move to a rework queue
- Escalate to admin

All issues are logged in the order history for complete traceability."

*Action: Show issue reporting*

---

**[6:30 - 7:30] Quality Check Process**

*Visual: QC approval workflow*

**Narrator:**
"Quality Check is a critical gate. When an order reaches QC, it undergoes inspection.

The QC team can:
- **Approve** - Order moves to packaging
- **Reject** - Order returns for rework
- **Hold** - Needs further review

Each decision requires notes. For rejections, specific issues must be documented so the production team knows what to fix.

This process ensures only quality products reach customers."

*Action: Demonstrate QC workflow*

---

**[7:30 - 8:00] Closing**

*Visual: Complete workflow summary*

**Narrator:**
"You now understand how orders flow through departments, how workers update progress, and how managers control the workflow. Next up: Reports and Analytics!"

- Screen resolution: 1920x1080
- Browser: Chrome (latest)
- Use demo data set (not production)
- Enable feature flags for all features

### Audio Requirements
- Professional voiceover
- Background music: subtle, professional
- Sound effects for clicks/transitions

### Graphics Needed
- Animated logo intro
- Lower thirds for feature names
- Arrow/highlight annotations
- Transition effects between sections

### Accessibility
- Include closed captions
- Describe visual elements for screen readers
- Use high contrast highlights
